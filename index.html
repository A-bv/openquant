Each future payment is discounted to today: PV = payment ÷ (1+rate)^years. Add them up and that's the "worth to you today" pile. The rate is how much a dollar now beats a dollar later — your impatience, or what you'd otherwise earn on the cash. This is the time value of money, the first idea in the EPFL Principles of Finance course.
